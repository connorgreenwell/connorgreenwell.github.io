About Me

Hi! I’m a PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Kentucky, where I am advised by Dr. Nathan Jacobs. My research interests intersect computer vision and remote sensing; specifically using deep learning and satellite imagery to study geo-temporal patterns in large scale collections of photographs. I earned a BS in Computer Science and Mathematics from the University of Kentucky in 2016. Previously I have interned at Oak Ridge National Laboratory and at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. I live in Lexington, KY with my wife Abbie, our two dogs Tesla and Hopper, and our cat Jellybean.
